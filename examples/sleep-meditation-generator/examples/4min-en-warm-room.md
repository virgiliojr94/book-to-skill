# Example — 4-minute English Sleep Meditation, Warm Room

This is the canonical example from the source guide. Use it as a quality benchmark for new generations.

**Parameters**: duration=4min, language=en, style=clinical-hypnotic, metaphor=warm-room

---

## Script

> **Recording note**: speak at ~85–95 wpm, leaving the indicated pauses. Voice close, warm, low, unforced. Music starts before the first word and continues after the last.

```
[music begins softly, warm ambient texture, no clear beat]

Welcome. There is nothing you need to solve right now.
There is nothing you need to prove to the night.
You can simply lie here, supported by the bed,
and allow this moment to become a little easier.

[pause 4 sec]

If sleep comes, it can come naturally.
If it takes its time, rest is still doing something kind for you.
So there is no need to chase sleep.
There is only the gentle practice of letting the body remember how to soften.

[pause 5 sec]

Notice the breath as it is.
You do not need to make it perfect.
Perhaps the next exhale can be just a little longer than the inhale,
like a quiet wave moving back from the shore.
Breathing in, receiving.
Breathing out, releasing.

[pause 6 sec]

Let the forehead become smooth.
Let the tiny muscles around the eyes loosen.
Let the jaw unhook, even slightly,
as if the face no longer needs to hold the day together.

[pause 5 sec]

And now the shoulders can drop by a few millimetres. That is enough.
The arms can become heavier.
The hands can rest without gripping anything,
without holding any thought,
without preparing for tomorrow.

[pause 6 sec]

Feel the chest moving gently.
Feel the belly soften under the breath.
Each exhale can carry away a little more effort.
Not all at once. Just enough for this moment.

[pause 5 sec]

Now imagine a quiet room at night.
The light is low and golden.
Outside, the world is slowing down.
Inside, everything is simple.
A soft blanket, a warm silence,
and the feeling that you are allowed to stop.

[pause 7 sec]

In this room, thoughts may still pass by,
but they no longer need your answer.
A thought can arrive, and drift.
Another thought can arrive, and drift.
Like small leaves moving on dark water,
they can move on without being followed.

[pause 7 sec]

The body knows more about sleep than the mind needs to understand.
The body has slept before. The body can find that path again.
You do not need to push it there.
You can only make the space gentle enough,
quiet enough, safe enough.

[pause 6 sec]

And perhaps, somewhere beneath the words,
a deeper rhythm is already beginning.
The rhythm of weight.
The rhythm of warmth.
The rhythm of letting the night hold you,
rather than trying to hold the night.

[pause 7 sec]

If you are still awake, that is completely okay.
You can rest here.
You can let the music continue.
You can let the breath become smaller, softer, slower,
until even listening becomes unnecessary.

[pause 6 sec]

Nothing to force. Nothing to follow.
Just the bed beneath you, the night around you,
and the quiet permission to rest.

[long fade, music continues 30–60 sec]
```

## Why This Works (annotation)

| Section | What it does well |
|---|---|
| Opening 4 lines | Removes performance pressure without naming "performance" — uses "nothing you need to solve" instead |
| "If sleep comes" passage | Directly addresses the core insomnia anti-pattern: chasing sleep |
| Breath section | Soft instruction ("perhaps the next exhale can be"), no command, paired with a metaphor anchor ("quiet wave moving back from the shore") |
| Body release | Sequential, slow, ends each region with permission ("That is enough") |
| Imagery | One metaphor (warm-room), three sensory anchors (low golden light, soft blanket, warm silence), no new scene |
| "Thoughts can drift" | Defuses rumination without commanding the listener to stop thinking |
| "The body knows more about sleep than the mind needs to understand" | Reframes the locus of control away from the conscious mind — classic permissive hypnotic move |
| Closing | "If you are still awake, that is completely okay" — full removal of pressure; no awakening language |

## Pair with Published Description (English)

> **Sleep Meditation — Warm Room** — a 4-minute guided sleep meditation with a warm musical bed.
>
> This audio supports relaxation and sleep readiness. It is **not** a medical treatment. The voice is permissive and uncommanding, so there is nothing to perform — just rest.
>
> **How to use it.** Listen in a safe resting place, lying down, with the lights low. Use earbuds at low volume or a soft speaker. You don't need to fall asleep during the track for it to be useful.
>
> **Please note.** If insomnia persists, or if you experience severe distress, breathing problems, or concerns about medication, please speak with a qualified clinician. This audio is not a substitute for CBT-I or medical evaluation.
