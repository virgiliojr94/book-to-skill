# Clinical & Ethical Principles

These are the **hard constraints** every generation must respect. They reflect AASM, NICE, European Insomnia Guideline 2023, Accademia Italiana di Medicina del Sonno, and Cochrane positions on insomnia, hypnosis, and music-supported relaxation.

## 1. CBT-I is first-line; this audio is adjunctive

Chronic insomnia is treated through cognitive and behavioural sleep medicine. CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioural Therapy for Insomnia) is the first-line intervention in all major clinical guidance. Hypnosis, meditation, and music are **adjunctive or supportive** tools that reduce cognitive and physiological arousal.

**Operational consequence**: never claim the track *treats* or *cures* insomnia. The legitimate frame is: "supports relaxation and sleep readiness."

## 2. Reduce arousal, not consciousness

The goal is not to force unconsciousness. It is to soften:
- Muscle tone
- Breath rate (longer exhale than inhale, gentle)
- Threat monitoring
- Mental rehearsal of the day or tomorrow

A track that tries to "knock the listener out" creates performance pressure and backfires.

## 3. Do not fight wakefulness

Insomnia is maintained by **effort, monitoring, performance anxiety**. The script must explicitly remove pressure by saying that **rest is already useful, even before sleep arrives**.

Sample language:
- "If sleep comes, it can come naturally. If it takes its time, rest is still doing something kind for you."
- "There is no need to chase sleep."

## 4. Permissive hypnosis (indirect language)

Indirect language respects autonomy and works better for online audiences (no clinician present to manage resistance).

| ✅ Use | ❌ Avoid |
|---|---|
| "you may notice" | "you will feel" |
| "perhaps" | "now" (as command) |
| "as if" | "you must" |
| "there is no need" | "go deeper immediately" |
| "you can" | "sleep now" |
| "if you wish" | "do this" |

## 5. One emotional promise: safety

Not magic. Not depth. Not control. **Safety.**

The track should return repeatedly to the felt sense of being safe, supported, and allowed to stop.

## 6. No cognitive activation

Forbidden in any sleep track:
- Complex stories or plots
- Problem-solving prompts
- Counting that feels like a task
- Vivid adventure / travel imagery
- Emotionally charged memories ("think of a happy time…")
- Alertness checks ("are you still with me?")
- Awakening language ("when you are ready, open your eyes")

## 7. One central metaphor

Pick one and sustain it. Don't change scene mid-track. Scene switching is cognitive load.

Acceptable central metaphors (all sensory, low-contrast, slow):
- Warm room at night
- Quiet shore with slow waves
- Soft forest at dusk
- Night meadow under a low moon
- Candlelit room with low light

## 8. Sensory imagery, not narrative

Describe **what is sensed** (warmth, weight, dimness, softness), not **what happens** (a walk, a journey, an event).

## 9. No pseudo-science

Forbidden:
- "Binaural beats heal insomnia"
- Specific Hz claims ("432 Hz solfeggio cures…")
- Subliminal messages
- Brainwave entrainment as therapy
- Crystal / chakra healing language
- Quantum / vibrational claims

Music-supported relaxation **is** evidence-aligned (Cochrane). Specific-frequency healing **is not**.

## 10. Hard limits — refuse these requests

| Request | Refuse / reframe |
|---|---|
| Advise stopping medication | Refuse. Replace with "speak with a qualified clinician." |
| Diagnose a sleep disorder | Refuse. The track is not diagnostic. |
| Use subliminal content | Refuse. Subliminal claims violate ethics + ad policy. |
| Claim permanent cure | Refuse. Reframe as "supports sleep readiness." |
| Add fear of being awake | Refuse. Anti-pattern that worsens insomnia. |
| Trauma-activating imagery | Refuse. Soften further; recommend clinician. |

## 11. Always include a clinical-referral line

Every published description must contain something equivalent to:

> "This audio supports relaxation and sleep readiness. It is not a medical treatment. If insomnia persists, or if you experience severe distress, breathing problems, or medication concerns, please speak with a qualified clinician."

Translate this when generating in IT / ES / FR. Never drop it.

## 12. Audio safety

- Integrated loudness **lower** than normal spoken content — users may be in bed with earbuds
- No sudden sound effects, drops, risers, cymbals, dramatic entrances
- Soft low end (no rumble), reduced 2–5 kHz harshness, no clicks, plosives, sharp transients
- No abrupt ending — always a slow fade

## 13. End without awakening language

This is a **sleep** track. The listener should be free to drift off without a callback. Do not include "when you are ready, open your eyes", "come back to the room", or any orientation back to wakefulness.
