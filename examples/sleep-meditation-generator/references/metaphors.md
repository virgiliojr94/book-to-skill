# Central Metaphors

Pick **one** per track. Sustain it. Do not switch. Each metaphor has **sensory anchors** the script binds to — never narrative actions.

A good central metaphor for sleep meditation is:
- **Slow** (no movement implying activity)
- **Warm** (parasympathetic association)
- **Dim** (visually low-stimulation)
- **Bounded** (a place, not a journey)
- **Safe** (no ambiguity, no threat)

---

## warm-room

A quiet room at night. Default and most universally safe.

**Sensory anchors**:
- Low golden light, perhaps a candle, perhaps a lamp far away
- A soft blanket, the texture of which is felt as weight
- Warm silence (not absence of sound — a *quality* of stillness)
- The feeling of being held by the room
- Walls that contain without confining
- Outside: the world is slowing down. Inside: everything is simple.

**Avoid**: doors opening, anyone entering, getting up, leaving the room.

**English example phrasing**:
> "Now imagine a quiet room at night. The light is low and golden. Outside, the world is slowing down. Inside, everything is simple. A soft blanket, a warm silence, and the feeling that you are allowed to stop."

**Italian example phrasing**:
> "Ora immagina una stanza tranquilla, di notte. La luce è bassa, dorata. Fuori, il mondo rallenta. Dentro, tutto è semplice. Una coperta morbida, un silenzio caldo, e la sensazione di poterti fermare."

---

## quiet-shore

A still shore at dusk. Water moving slowly back and forth without drama.

**Sensory anchors**:
- The slow rhythm of small waves, never breaking
- A warm fading sky, no clear horizon
- Sand that holds the body without being noticed
- The feeling of the tide doing the work of letting go
- Salt-warm air, not cold, not damp
- No boats, no people, no noise — only the rhythm of returning

**Avoid**: storms, swimming, distant lights, anything moving toward the listener.

**Bind to breath**: "like a quiet wave moving back from the shore" pairs naturally with "exhale longer than inhale."

---

## soft-forest

A forest at dusk. Light filtered, air still.

**Sensory anchors**:
- Slow dimming light through leaves above
- Soft moss or earth beneath, holding the body
- No wind, or only the gentlest hint of air
- Distant sounds so faint they may not be sounds at all
- A sense of being surrounded but not watched
- The forest itself is breathing, slowly

**Avoid**: walking, animals approaching, dark spaces between trees that imply hiding, anything fast.

---

## night-meadow

A meadow under a low moon. Open but bounded by quiet.

**Sensory anchors**:
- Tall grass, very still
- A low warm moon, not a cold bright one
- The horizon blurred into evening
- Body resting on the meadow, the ground warm
- Stars perhaps appearing, slowly, one at a time
- A sense of the night holding the whole field gently

**Avoid**: shooting stars, weather, anything dramatic. The meadow is **not** a story.

---

## candle-room

A small room with a single candle, very low. Most intimate of the set.

**Sensory anchors**:
- One small flame, warm and unmoving
- The boundary of the light is soft
- Beyond the candle: gentle darkness, not threat
- Slow warmth on the face
- The candle is in no hurry — it has all night
- The listener is in no hurry either

**Avoid**: the candle going out (anti-pattern: implies loss), flickering wildly, anyone else lighting it.

---

## custom (user-provided)

If the user supplies their own metaphor, validate it against the 5 criteria above (slow, warm, dim, bounded, safe).

**Reject and reframe**:
- "Flying" → not bounded, implies effort
- "Swimming" → activity
- "A journey" → not bounded
- "A childhood memory" → emotionally charged
- "A spaceship" → cognitive activation
- "A mountain summit" → effort, height, drama
- "A waterfall" → high-energy sensory load

**Reframe pattern**: "I can use a related slower image — instead of [user's metaphor], how about [closest safe variant]?"

---

## Binding the Metaphor to the Script

For the chosen metaphor, **bind ~3 sensory anchors per minute of imagery section**. Repeat them. Repetition is the point. Do not introduce new sensory information after the 75% mark of the track.

| Time in imagery section | Action |
|---|---|
| First 30% | Introduce the metaphor with 1–2 anchors |
| Middle 40% | Layer in 1–2 more anchors, weave with breath/permission |
| Last 30% | Only repeat existing anchors, no new ones |
