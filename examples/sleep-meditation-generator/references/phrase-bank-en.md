# English Phrase Bank

Permissive, low-cognitive-load phrases organised by script section. Pick **1–3 per section**, bind to the chosen metaphor, separate with pauses. Never use more than is needed.

---

## Section 1 — Orientation (safety, no pressure)

- Welcome.
- There is nothing you need to solve right now.
- There is nothing you need to prove to the night.
- You can simply lie here, supported by the bed.
- Allow this moment to become a little easier.
- You are allowed to stop.
- This time is yours, and there is nothing to do.
- The day is finished. Whatever remained, it can wait.
- You don't have to perform anything here.

## Section 2 — Breath & body settling

- Notice the breath as it is.
- You do not need to make it perfect.
- Perhaps the next exhale can be just a little longer than the inhale.
- Like a quiet wave moving back from the shore.
- Breathing in, receiving. Breathing out, releasing.
- Let the breath find its own rhythm.
- The body knows how to breathe. You can let it.
- Each exhale can carry away a little more of the day.
- Feel the weight of the body resting into the bed.

## Section 3 — Progressive release (face → jaw → shoulders → hands → chest → belly → legs)

- Let the forehead become smooth.
- Let the tiny muscles around the eyes loosen.
- Let the jaw unhook, even slightly.
- As if the face no longer needs to hold the day together.
- The shoulders can drop by a few millimetres. That is enough.
- The arms can become heavier.
- The hands can rest without gripping anything.
- Without holding any thought. Without preparing for tomorrow.
- Feel the chest moving gently.
- Feel the belly soften under the breath.
- Each exhale can carry away a little more effort.
- The legs can rest. The feet can rest.
- Not all at once. Just enough for this moment.

## Section 4 — Guided imagery & permissive hypnosis (bind to metaphor)

- Now imagine a [metaphor anchor]…
- The light is low and golden.
- Outside, the world is slowing down.
- Inside, everything is simple.
- A soft blanket, a warm silence, the feeling that you are allowed to stop.
- Thoughts may still pass by, but they no longer need your answer.
- A thought can arrive, and drift. Another thought can arrive, and drift.
- Like small leaves moving on dark water, they can move on without being followed.
- The body knows more about sleep than the mind needs to understand.
- The body has slept before. The body can find that path again.
- You do not need to push it there.
- You can only make the space gentle enough, quiet enough, safe enough.
- Perhaps a deeper rhythm is already beginning.
- The rhythm of weight. The rhythm of warmth.
- The rhythm of letting the night hold you, rather than trying to hold the night.

## Section 5 — Sleep permission & fade

- If you are still awake, that is completely okay.
- You can rest here.
- You can let the music continue.
- You can let the breath become smaller, softer, slower.
- Until even listening becomes unnecessary.
- Nothing to force. Nothing to follow.
- Just the bed beneath you, the night around you, and the quiet permission to rest.
- Rest is enough.
- The rest of this is just music.

## Forbidden Phrases (do not generate)

- "Sleep now."
- "You must sleep."
- "Go deeper immediately."
- "When you are ready, open your eyes."
- "Come back to the room."
- "Imagine yourself flying / running / walking on the beach with friends."
- "Think about a happy memory."
- "Are you still with me?"
- "Count down from 10."
- "Repeat after me."
- Anything making a medical promise ("This will cure your insomnia", "You will sleep tonight").

## Phrasing Patterns to Reuse

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Permission framing | "You can…", "You may…", "Perhaps…" |
| Removing performance | "There is no need to…" |
| Sensory anchor | "Notice the…", "Feel the…" |
| Soft instruction | "Let the…" (not "make the…") |
| Conditional safety | "If [X], that is completely okay." |
| Restful absolution | "[Action] is enough." |
| Triadic repetition | "X, Y, and Z." (use sparingly — closes the breath cycle) |
