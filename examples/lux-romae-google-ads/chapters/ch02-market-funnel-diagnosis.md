# Chapter 2: Market & Funnel Diagnosis

## Core Idea
Rome demand is record-strong but the STR market is dense and price-transparent; Lux Romae's funnel has clear differentiators (location, design, flexible policies) but probable friction in conversion path. **Win on relevance, trust, and friction reduction — not reach.**

## Frameworks Introduced

- **Year-Round-with-Peaks Seasonality Model**: Rome is **not** a summer-only destination. Bid/budget for spring, early summer, autumn, Christmas/New Year, and event weeks — not for one big summer push.
  - AirDNA seasonality score: 65 (moderate, not extreme)
  - Eurostat: European tourism nights still peak July–August
  - Academic spatiotemporal work: spring particularly popular in city-regions like Rome

- **Event Compression Detection**: Rome is highly event-sensitive — Pope Francis' funeral (25 Apr 2025) drove occupancy 88.8%, ADR €351.13; forward occupancy for conclave already >84%.
  - Operational rule: weekly event + occupancy calendar review; loosen ROAS/CPA targets **before** demand spikes, not during

- **Three-Layer Competition Model**:
  1. Hotels / aparthotels / premium apartments (HVS: stable supply + further luxury openings)
  2. ~42,000 short-term rentals on Airbnb + Vrbo (AirDNA Rome overview)
  3. OTAs and metasearch (Google explicitly says hotel ads can be shown by suppliers, OTAs, and metasearch)

- **Brand-Leakage Risk Framework**: Lux Romae is publicly distributed on Airbnb, Vrbo, Houfy → branded paid traffic can leak to intermediaries. Branded Search defence is **mandatory**, not optional.

## Key Concepts

- **Rome 2025 record**: 22.9M arrivals, 52.92M overnight stays (Turismo Roma)
- **Hotel occupancy 2024**: consistently >70%; rates ~30% above 2019 in real terms
- **Rome STR base**: 42,376 properties on Airbnb + Vrbo; 64% average occupancy; ADR >$200 (AirDNA free overview)
- **"Official site" badge**: Google awards it to direct suppliers in hotel surfaces; OTAs ineligible
- **Assisted enquiry**: a contact via WhatsApp/email/phone that converts off-site after the paid visit

## Funnel Strengths (Lux Romae public assets)
- Newly renovated, elegant apartment positioning (Ostiense/Piramide)
- Address: Via Giovanni da Empoli 6 — near Piramide Metro and Ostiense Station
- Multiple rate policies: **Flexible, Best Price, Reserve, Family Flexible**
- Mix of live rate info + email/WhatsApp assisted contact
- Family + couple appeal, refined visual presentation

## Funnel Weaknesses (likely)
- Assisted contact CTAs alongside rates → probable mix of instant + later-close bookings
- Without offline conversion import, the later-close share is invisible to bidding
- 13-language site without paid-side language discipline → fragmentation risk

## Mental Models

- **"Loosen targets before peaks, not during them."** Rome event compression appears fast; if you wait, you've already missed cheap clicks.
- **"You compete with three layers, not one."** Defend brand against OTAs, fight non-brand against hotels + apartments + other STRs.
- **"Public differentiators are the ad copy brief."** Ostiense/Piramide transport, 2BR family suitability, design aesthetic, flexible policies → these become headlines, callouts, sitelinks.

## Anti-patterns

- **Treating Rome as a summer-only market** → underbid in spring, autumn, Christmas
- **Ignoring event calendars** → miss compression weeks, overpay when everyone else wakes up
- **Skipping branded defence** because "they'll find us anyway" → OTAs siphon your own demand at lower-funnel
- **Optimising only to instant bookings** when traffic actually closes via WhatsApp → kills profitable campaigns that look "broken"

## Key Takeaways

1. Build a **rolling event/occupancy calendar** (Turismo Roma events page is a live source).
2. Plan for **peaks across multiple seasons**, not one.
3. **Brand defence is non-negotiable** given OTA distribution.
4. Until the booking flow is verified, treat the account as **hybrid commerce + lead-gen**.
5. Mirror Lux Romae's public differentiators (location, transit, design, family-friendly, flexible policies) in **every ad and landing-page hero**.
6. Plan for **price visibility** — guests can comparison-shop you instantly, so the value prop must compete on more than rate.
