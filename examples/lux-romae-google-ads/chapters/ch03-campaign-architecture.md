# Chapter 3: Campaign Architecture & Budget Scenarios

## Core Idea
Sequence campaigns by **incremental booking efficiency, not by Google's product menu**. Search and Vacation Rentals lead; PMax and video remarketing follow only after measurement is clean.

## Frameworks Introduced

### Recommended Campaign Mix

| Layer | Role | Budget priority | Status | Why for Lux Romae |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Search brand defence** | Stop OTA leakage on branded queries | Very high | Launch immediately | Lowest-funnel, should be cheap and protective |
| **Search high-intent non-brand** | Capture family/location/apartment intent | Very high | Launch immediately | Best incremental direct-booking source |
| **Search remarketing lists** | Re-close date-checkers and abandoners | High | After audiences populate | Very efficient on small budgets |
| **Display remarketing** | Stay visible to abandoners | Medium | After tracking stable | Recovery only — not cold prospecting |
| **YouTube / Video remarketing** | Trust & recall to warm audiences | Medium-low | Add later | Low-cost nurture, not cold-start |
| **Performance Max** | Expand beyond exact keyword inventory | Medium | Only after reliable data | Powerful, but wastes budget without signals |
| **Local / store-goal PMax** | Footfall actions | Low | Usually avoid | Wrong objective for online lodging |
| **Google Vacation Rentals / Hotel Center** | High-intent travel inventory on Google | Very high if feasible | Parallel to Search | Most efficient Google-native lodging layer |

### Search-vs-PMax Coexistence (deterministic)
> Google: when a user's query is **identical** to an eligible Search keyword in the account, the Search campaign is prioritised over Performance Max.

**Use this**: pin every high-value exact and phrase query in Search → PMax only works around the edges. This prevents PMax from cannibalising your most efficient demand.

### Budget Scenarios

| Scenario | Monthly | Split |
|---|---|---|
| **Lean validation** | €600–1,200 | 70–80% Search / 15–25% remarketing / 0–10% PMax test |
| **Controlled growth** | €1,200–2,500 | 60–70% Search / 15–20% remarketing / 10–20% PMax or travel feed |
| **Peak-season push** | €2,500–5,000 | 45–60% Search / 10–15% remarketing / 20–35% PMax or travel feed / 5–10% video remarketing |

**Strong opinion** (justified): until offline import of assisted bookings is clean, allocate **no more than a small test share** to PMax. Google itself recommends ≥6 weeks of stable run and minimal early changes — with thin data that becomes expensive ambiguity, not learning.

### Campaign-Type Comparison (where each wins / loses)

| Type | Wins | Loses | Best Lux Romae use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Search | Strongest intent, precise control | Limited scale if query volumes thin | Brand defence, long-tail location, family/couple intent, policy messaging |
| PMax | Cross-channel reach, automation, incremental demand | Drifts to weak inventory with weak signals | Secondary layer after clean conversion data |
| Display | Cheap impressions, strong remarketing | Weak cold prospecting on small budgets | Abandoned visitors only |
| Video | Memory, trust | Hard to justify cold on tiny budgets | Remarketing only, host-led / apartment-led trust creative |
| Local / store-goal | Footfall | Wrong objective for online lodging | Use location assets instead |
| Vacation Rentals / Hotel | Very high intent, direct booking links in Google travel surfaces | Needs feed/partner support + accurate price/availability | Priority if booking engine supports it |

## Key Concepts

- **Brand defence**: paid Search on your property name to prevent OTAs/aggregators winning your branded clicks
- **Eligible keyword**: a Search keyword in your account that could legitimately match the user's query and is not paused/blocked
- **PMax learning period**: Google's recommended ≥6-week stabilisation window with minimal changes
- **Travel feed**: structured listing/price/availability feed (or crawled listings) used for Google Hotel/Vacation Rentals eligibility

## Mental Models

- **"Pin the demand, fence the edges."** Search pins what you know converts; PMax explores edges where Search has no keyword.
- **"Budget is a measurement-quality function."** Allow PMax/feed only as much spend as your conversion signal can supervise.
- **"Peak push is reactive, not standing."** The €2,500–5,000 tier is for confirmed peaks (Easter, conclave-style events, Christmas, autumn shoulder) — not the default monthly state.

## Anti-patterns

- **Mirrored Search + PMax + Display on day 1 with equal budget** → PMax eats Search's queries that aren't pinned; Display burns budget on cold inventory
- **Treating PMax as a Search alternative** when budget is small → it's an expansion layer, not a replacement
- **Running local / store-goal campaigns** for an online-booked apartment → optimised for visits/calls/directions, not bookings
- **Spinning up a feed without verified booking engine support** → broken price/availability → ad disapprovals and trust damage

## Key Takeaways

1. **Launch only the "very high" priority lines first**: brand defence, non-brand high-intent, and Vacation Rentals if eligible.
2. **Pin keywords in Search** before letting PMax explore.
3. **Hold PMax budget to a test share** until offline import is verified.
4. **Display = remarketing only.** No broad prospecting at this budget.
5. **Use peak push only for confirmed compression weeks**, not as the default budget tier.
6. **Vacation Rentals / Hotel Center is the highest-leverage Google-native lodging layer** — check booking engine support early.
