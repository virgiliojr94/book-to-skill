# Chapter 1: Executive Summary & Core Thesis

## Core Idea
For a one-property direct-booking business in a record-strong but dense market, the winning Google Ads route is **narrow and disciplined**: brand protection + high-intent non-brand Search + remarketing + Google's travel inventory — not Performance Max as a starting point.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **4-Priority Architecture**: brand defence Search, high-intent non-brand Search, remarketing, Google Vacation Rentals / Hotel Center connectivity.
  - When to use: any single-property STR with a meaningful direct-booking ambition
  - How: sequence them in this order; PMax enters only after reliable booking data exists

- **Sequencing Rule (Search-first)**: Search carries the account in early phases; PMax is a controlled expansion layer later.
  - Rationale: PMax needs ≥6 weeks and avoidance of frequent early changes to learn (Google's own recommendation). With thin data, that becomes expensive ambiguity.

- **Conversion-first, not awareness-first**: market is high-demand but high-competition and price-transparent — bid for intent, not reach.

- **Hybrid Commerce + Lead-Gen Recognition**: the public Lux Romae booking page mixes live rate info with email/WhatsApp contact CTAs. Treat the account as **both** instant booking and qualified lead-style enquiry until the exact checkout flow is verified.

## Key Concepts

- **Brand protection / brand defence**: paid Search on your own property name to prevent OTAs/aggregators from intercepting your highest-intent demand
- **Google Vacation Rentals**: Google product surfacing vacation rentals in dedicated travel search; supports free booking links + paid hotel campaign formats
- **"Official site" badge**: Google badge available to direct suppliers in hotel surfaces; OTAs do **not** qualify
- **Assisted booking**: a booking that completes off-site (WhatsApp, email, phone) after a paid visit — invisible to Smart Bidding without offline import

## Mental Models

- **"Pin the demand you already have, then expand."** Brand and exact-match non-brand are demand you can prove; PMax and broad are exploration. Never explore before you own the proven demand.
- **"Search vs PMax is not a fight if you pin keywords."** When the query exactly matches an eligible Search keyword, Search wins over PMax in the same account. Use this deterministically.
- **"Google travel surfaces > generic display, for lodging."** Vacation Rentals / Hotel campaigns put Lux Romae alongside OTAs on Google's highest-intent lodging UI, often with the official-site badge that OTAs cannot earn.

## Anti-patterns

- **Launching 13 equal-language campaigns** because the site is in 13 languages → low data density per campaign, weak ad testing, slow Quality Score formation.
- **Turning on PMax immediately** with no offline conversion import → bidding optimises to phantom or shallow events, budget drifts into low-quality inventory.
- **Optimising to phone clicks or date-checks as primary** → shallow events misdirect Smart Bidding. Keep them secondary.
- **Trusting Google to "improvise" language matching** because the site is multilingual → mismatched ad/landing/booking-engine language tanks conversion rate.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Start with tightly controlled Search**, build measurement before scale.
2. **Match ad language to landing page language deliberately** — don't let Google improvise.
3. **Import every offline booking** that originates from paid traffic — without this, assisted bookings look unprofitable.
4. **Use Google travel-specific inventory** as soon as technical eligibility is confirmed; it can be the most efficient direct-booking layer.
5. **PMax is a later layer, not a starting point** for a one-property budget.
6. **Branded Search defence is mandatory**, not optional — Lux Romae already appears on Airbnb, Vrbo, Houfy.
